Meerkat

Meerkats form groups which are called "mob", "gang" or "clan". A meerkat clan often contains about 20 meerkats, but some super-families have 50 or more members.

  • Scientific Name: Suricata suricatta
  • Average Length: 35 to 50 centimetres with an added tail length of around 25 centimetres
  • Average Lifespan: 12 to 14 years in captivity, about half of this in the wild
  • Habitat: All parts of the Kalahari Desert in Botswana, in much of the Namib Desert in Namimbia and southwestern Angola, and in South Africa.

The meerkat is a small carnivoran belonging to the mongose family. It took its name by the same dutch word which means "lake cat" in Dutch.
